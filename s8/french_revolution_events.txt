The major historical events that led to the French Revolution are as follows:

1. Storming of the Bastille on July 14, 1789: This event marked the beginning of the revolution and symbolized the overthrow of the monarchy.

2. Abolition of feudal rights on August 4, 1789: This decree abolished the privileges of the nobility and clergy, leading to greater equality among the French population.

3. Declaration of the French Republic on September 22, 1792: This marked the end of the monarchy and the establishment of a republic in France.

4. Reign of Terror from 1793 to 1794: This period was characterized by mass executions and political repression as the revolutionary government sought to eliminate its enemies.

5. Rise of Napoleon Bonaparte in 1799: Napoleon seized power and established a new government, marking the end of the revolution.

These events, along with others, contributed to the social, political, and economic upheaval that defined the French Revolution.
